Motivation

By 2022, the United States will face a shortage of up to 135,000 geoscientists, potentially undermining its worldwide leadership in the geosciences and interrelated STEM fields. However, two well-documented leaks in the pipeline delivering qualified geoscientists involve retention of students already enrolled in undergraduate programs, and eventual facilitation of their job placement. Identified reasons for these leaks include limited opportunities for students to: (1) strengthen their identification with a geoscience career; (2) understand the variety of available career options; (3) find a career that best fits their interests and skills; and (4) become better prepared to handle professional responsibilities. These concerns are exacerbated for students from historically underserved racial and ethnic minorities, first-generation, and low-income backgrounds, who may have limited access to role models, or to high impact pedagogical strategies that serve them well.
