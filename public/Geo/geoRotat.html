HOW WILL IT WORK?

The program will utilize a “Medical School” approach with participants rotated through semester-long academic and professional career experiences. A total of 18 students will be initialy invited to participate in the first academic rotation, beginning January 2017.

From those who satisfactorily complete the academic rotation, 9 students will be chosen by lottery to continue into the two professional rotations. The 9 selected students will submit their order of priority for professional fields (energy, water, environmental). Selections will then be made by weighting priority and availability (only 3 participants in each field/rotation, fields cannot be repeated). All 18 students will participate in evaluation surveys/interviews and end-of-rotation presentations.

ACADEMIC ROTATION

Students will be assigned to a faculty mentor and participate in their research group activities.

In addition, they will attend seminars, presentations, and activities aimed at improving both academic performance and professional career preparedness, as illustrated below.
